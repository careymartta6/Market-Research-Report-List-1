<p><p>The Electronic Grade Trichlorosilane market primarily comprises two production processes: Direct Chlorination (DC) and Hydrochlorination (HC). The DC process involves the direct reaction of silicon with chlorine, yielding high purity trichlorosilane suitable for semiconductor and solar applications. In contrast, the HC process utilizes hydrogen and silicones to produce trichlorosilane, often resulting in lower impurities. Both methods play critical roles in meeting the increasing demand for high-quality silicon materials in electronics and photovoltaic industries.</p></p>
